# Iteration

The universe evolves by discrete iteration indexed by $n$. The iteration
index is not modal time. Rather, it records the global order in which
actualized events are processed. Each carrier advances along its own
modal‑time parameter, whereas pruning and actualization occur in a single
global sequence.

At iteration $n$, every ray in the current possibility space $P_n$
advances along its own modal‑time parameter, thereby generating new
waypoints and revealing potential geometric intersections. A ray bundle
associated with a set of carriers becomes resolvable only when all rays
in that bundle have progressed sufficiently far in their respective
modal times to reach a shared geometric intersection point. Until that
condition is satisfied, the actualization operator cannot act on that
bundle.

Once a bundle is resolvable, actualization selects an event

$$
e_n = A(P_n).
$$

Pruning then removes all rays incompatible with $e_n$:

$$
P_{n+1} = P(P_n, e_n),
$$

and actuality is updated according to

$$
A_{n+1} = A_n \cup \{e_n\}.
$$

Universal iteration and modal progression are therefore coordinated but
not synchronized: iteration proceeds globally, while each carrier’s modal
time advances locally and independently. Actualization occurs only when
modal progression has brought all participating rays to a common
geometric intersection.

The framework therefore specifies not only the structure of admissible
futures, but also the algorithm by which events are selected and
actualized. Modal time governs the internal progression of carriers,
while iteration governs the global ordering of actualizations. Their
coordination defines the dynamics of the universe.

---

## Structural Role

Iteration provides the global temporal ordering of actualized events. It:

- advances the universe one event at a time,
- coordinates pruning and actualization,
- and determines when modal futures become resolvable.

Modal time governs local waypoint progression; iteration governs global
event progression.

---

## Relations

- Iteration orders the sequence $(A_n, P_n)$.
- Modal progression is independent for each carrier.
- A resolvable set requires modal‑time alignment at a geometric
  intersection.
- Actualization produces $e_n$; pruning updates $P_{n+1}$.
- Iteration and modal time jointly determine the universe’s dynamics.
