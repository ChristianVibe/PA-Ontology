# Waypoint

A waypoint is the modal state of a carrier at a given position along one
of its rays. For a carrier $c$, each waypoint $w_n$ belongs to exactly
one ray in $R_c$ and encodes the full modal information of $c$ at that
modal‑time step.

The relation between waypoints and spacetime depends on the type of
carrier. For particles, waypoints are associated with spacetime points
through their progression in proper time. For quanta, which possess no
proper‑time evolution, waypoints exist purely in modal time and do not,
in general, correspond to spacetime locations except insofar as they
participate in actualized emission or absorption events.

Accordingly, geometric intersection conditions are evaluated directly on
particle waypoints, while contributions from quanta enter only through
the event structure in which they participate.

---

## Structural Role

Waypoints provide the discrete modal‑time representation of a carrier’s
admissible futures. They determine:

- the modal state of the carrier at each step,
- the geometric path along which compatibility is evaluated,
- and the points at which interactions may become available.

For particles, waypoints connect modal progression to spacetime
structure. For quanta, waypoints remain purely modal except at actual
events.

---

## Relations

- Each waypoint belongs to exactly one ray $r \in R_c$.
- Waypoints are ordered by modal time along each ray.
- Particle waypoints correspond to spacetime points via proper‑time
  progression.
- Quantum waypoints correspond to spacetime only at emission or
  absorption events.
- Actualization selects events when particle waypoints reach geometric
  intersections.
- Pruning removes rays whose waypoints are incompatible with newly
  actualized events.
