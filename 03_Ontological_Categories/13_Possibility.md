# Possibility

Possibility is the set $P$ of admissible modal futures of the universe.
Let $C$ denote the set of carriers and let $R_c$ be the ray bundle of
carrier $c$. Then the possibility space is the structured product

$$
P = \prod_{c \in C} R_c.
$$

This is not a Hilbert‑space tensor product. It carries no linear,
inner‑product, or probabilistic structure. An element of $P$ selects one
admissible ray for each carrier.

Because each selected ray advances in its own modal time, a joint modal
configuration is defined only for tuples of modal‑time values at which
the corresponding waypoints project to a common spacetime location,
thereby making a joint event admissible.

Importantly, $P$ is not itself a physically constrained space. It may
contain mutually incompatible futures and is not restricted a priori by
locality, causal structure, or conservation laws. Those constraints are
imposed only through pruning and actualization. Thus $P$ represents what
could occur in the modal sense, not what is physically realized.

---

## Structural Role

Possibility provides the full modal landscape of admissible futures for
the universe. It determines:

- the set of rays available to each carrier,
- the modal progression of waypoints,
- the geometric intersections that may become actualizable,
- and the space in which compatibility and pruning operate.

Possibility is the domain of modal evolution, independent of physical
constraints until actualization imposes them.

---

## Relations

- The universe is the ordered pair $(A, P)$.
- Each carrier contributes its ray bundle $R_c$ to the product structure
  of $P$.
- Modal time governs progression within $P$.
- Actualization selects admissible intersections from $P$ and adds events
  to $A$.
- Pruning removes incompatible rays from $P$ based on newly actualized
  events.
- Joint modal configurations exist only when waypoints from selected rays
  coincide at a common spacetime location.
