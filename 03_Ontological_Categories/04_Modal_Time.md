# Modal Time

Modal time is the intrinsic ordering parameter on admissible futures in
the realm of possibility $P$. It is not a physical time coordinate and
imposes no global temporal foliation. Instead, it provides the local
sequencing of waypoints along each carrier’s ray.

For a ray

$$
r = (w_0, w_1, w_2, \ldots),
$$

the waypoints are ordered by increasing modal time. For particles, modal
time is aligned with proper time but is not identical to it. For quanta,
modal time is the only progression parameter.

Modal time is therefore a purely modal ordering. It governs waypoint
progression, compatibility evaluation, and pruning. Because it is
defined locally on each carrier and depends only on that carrier’s
intrinsic modal structure, it is Lorentz invariant in the restricted
sense that different inertial observers may assign different spacetime
coordinates to the same process, but they agree on the ordering of
waypoints along a given carrier.

Modal progression occurs at the invariant causal speed for all carriers,
ensuring that waypoint ordering respects the causal structure associated
with realized spacetime events.

---

## Structural Role

Modal time provides the internal temporal ordering of admissible futures
in $P$. It determines:

- the progression of waypoints along each ray,
- when geometric intersections become available,
- when compatibility can be evaluated,
- and how pruning propagates through the possibility structure.

It is the temporal backbone of the realm of possibility, independent of
spacetime coordinates and global iteration.

---

## Relations

- Modal time lives entirely in the realm of possibility $P$.
- Proper time applies only to particles in actuality $A$.
- Iteration $n$ orders global actualization steps, distinct from modal
  progression.
- Spacetime time is assigned to actualized events after they occur.
- All carriers advance in modal time; only particles also advance in
  proper time.
