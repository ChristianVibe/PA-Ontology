# Carrier

A carrier $c$ is an entity characterized by:

- a finite ray bundle $R_c$,
- a causal chain $\pi_c$ over the events in which it participates,
- an intrinsic modal length $\lambda_c$,
- a waypoint density $\rho_c = 1/\lambda_c$,
- and modal‑time progression.

Carriers are partitioned into two classes: **particles** and **quanta**.

---

## Structural Role

Carriers are the fundamental ontic entities that span both realms of the
universe:

- In possibility $P$, a carrier is represented by its ray bundle $R_c$
  and modal progression.
- In actuality $A$, a carrier is represented by its causal chain $\pi_c$
  linking the events in which it participates.

The intrinsic modal length $\lambda_c$ determines waypoint spacing along
each ray, and the waypoint density $\rho_c$ determines how finely the
carrier’s admissible futures are represented.

Carriers provide the structural backbone for both modal evolution and
actualized event structure.

---

## Relations

- Each carrier has a ray bundle $R_c$ in the realm of possibility $P$.
- Each carrier has a causal chain $\pi_c$ in the realm of actuality $A$.
- Modal time governs the progression of waypoints along each ray.
- Particles and quanta differ in their temporal structure and causal‑chain
  properties.
- Actualization extends the causal chain of every carrier participating
  in an event.
- Pruning removes rays from $R_c$ that are incompatible with newly
  actualized events.
