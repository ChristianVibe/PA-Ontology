# Particle

A particle $p$ is a carrier satisfying:

- it has proper‑time progression;
- its causal chain $\pi_p$ is a strict total order on the set of all events involving $p$;
- $|\pi_p| \ge 1$ and may be unbounded;
- waypoint spacing is determined by its Compton length $\lambda_C$;
- the Compton length satisfies $\lambda_C \propto 1/m_p$, so the waypoint density
  $$
  \rho_p = \frac{1}{\lambda_C}
  $$
  is proportional to the particle’s mass;
- waypoint density determines emergent mass.

---

## Structural Role

Particles are carriers with full proper‑time evolution and unbounded causal chains.
They provide the persistent, worldline‑based structure of actuality:

- proper time orders their events,
- waypoint density encodes their mass,
- and their ray bundles represent admissible futures in possibility.

Particles anchor the ontology’s reconstruction of spacetime and supply the long‑lived
entities that participate in interactions across many iterations.

---

## Relations

- Particles are one of the two carrier classes, the other being quanta.
- Each particle has a ray bundle $R_p$ in possibility and a causal chain $\pi_p$ in actuality.
- Proper time applies only to particles; quanta evolve only in modal time.
- Actualization extends $\pi_p$ whenever the particle participates in an event.
- Pruning removes rays from $R_p$ that are incompatible with newly actualized events.
