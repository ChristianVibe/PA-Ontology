# Per‑Carrier Causal Order

Each carrier $c$ is associated with a causal chain

$$
\pi_c,
$$

which orders the events in which that carrier participates. The specific
structure of $\pi_c$ depends on the carrier type:

- for particles, $\pi_p$ is a strict total order over all events involving $p$;
- for quanta, $\pi_q$ contains only the emission event or the emission–absorption pair.

---

## Structural Role

The per‑carrier causal chain provides the temporal and causal ordering of
events in the realm of actuality. It determines:

- how each carrier’s history is structured,
- how events involving that carrier are sequenced,
- and how spacetime relations are reconstructed from actualized events.

Particles have extended causal chains, reflecting persistent worldlines.
Quanta have minimal causal chains, reflecting their short‑lived,
interaction‑mediating nature.

---

## Relations

- Every carrier has a causal chain $\pi_c$ in actuality $A$.
- Actualization extends $\pi_c$ whenever the carrier participates in an event.
- Particles have unbounded causal chains ordered by proper time.
- Quanta have one‑ or two‑event causal chains ordered by modal progression.
- Causal chains contribute to the reconstruction of spacetime relations among events.
