# Actualization Operator

The actualization operator selects one admissible interaction from the
current possibility structure and produces an event

$$
e_n = A(P_n).
$$

If

$$
e_n = (P_{e_n}, x_{e_n}),
$$

then:

- each carrier in $P_{e_n}$ actualizes at $x_{e_n}$,
- the causal chain of each participating carrier is extended,
- and $e_n$ is added to actuality.

The operator $A$ is stochastic: every admissible candidate in the
relevant resolvable set is assigned a normalized, nonzero weight.

---

## Structural Role

The actualization operator governs the discrete growth of actuality. It:

- selects one compatible interaction from the admissible modal futures,
- produces a new event in $A$,
- extends the causal chains of participating carriers,
- and triggers pruning of incompatible futures in $P$.

Actualization is the mechanism by which modal possibilities become
realized events.

---

## Relations

- Actualization maps the possibility structure $P_n$ to a new event
  $e_n$ in actuality $A$.
- Each event extends the causal chains $\pi_c$ of participating carriers.
- Pruning reshapes $P_{n+1}$ based on the newly actualized event.
- The stochastic weights assigned by $A$ determine which admissible
  interaction becomes realized.
- Actualization and pruning jointly drive the evolution of the universe
  $(A_n, P_n)$.
