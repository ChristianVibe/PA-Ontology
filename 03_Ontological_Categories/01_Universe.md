# Universe

The **universe** in the P/A ontology consists of two realms:



$$
U_n = (A_n, P_n),
$$



where:

- **Actuality $A_n$** contains the actualized events and their causal
  relations.
- **Possibility $P_n$** contains the admissible modal futures encoded
  as ray bundles and waypoints.

The index $n$ labels the **global iteration step**. Each iteration
updates both realms through the two fundamental operators:

- **Actualization $\mathcal{A}$**  
  Selects one admissible interaction from the current possibility
  structure and produces a new event in actuality.

- **Pruning $\mathcal{P}$**  
  Removes all modal futures incompatible with the newly actualized
  event, enforcing global consistency.

Together, these operators generate the dynamical evolution:

$$
(A_n, P_n) \xrightarrow{\;\mathcal{A},\,\mathcal{P}\;} (A_{n+1}, P_{n+1}).
$$



### Structural Role

The universe is the **top‑level ontological object**. It is not a
container for particles or fields; instead, it is the evolving pair of:

- what **is** (actuality), and  
- what **could be** (possibility).

All other ontological categories — carriers, rays, waypoints,
compatibility, entanglement — exist only as structured components of
$A$ and $P$.

### Relations

- **Actuality** and **possibility** are mutually constraining:
  actualization selects from possibility, and pruning reshapes
  possibility based on actuality.
- **Iteration** provides the global ordering of events.
- **Modal time** governs local progression within possibility.
- **Carriers** populate both realms: they have ray bundles in $P$ and
  causal chains in $A$.

### Key Quote (Appendix C)

> “The universe consists of two realms: the realm of actuality $A_n$,
> containing actualized events and their causal structure, and the realm
> of possibility $P_n$, containing admissible modal futures encoded as
> ray bundles and waypoints.”
