# Quantum

A quantum $q$ is a carrier satisfying:

- it has no proper‑time progression;
- its causal chain contains one or two events,
  $$
  \pi_q = \{e_{\mathrm{emit}}\}
  \quad\text{or}\quad
  \pi_q = \{e_{\mathrm{emit}}, e_{\mathrm{absorb}}\};
  $$
- it participates in at most two events;
- waypoint density determines emergent energy;
- it progresses only in modal time;
- its modal progression occurs at the invariant causal speed.

Quanta mediate transformations in the modal and actual states of
particles. Their modal‑time propagation enforces causal consistency
without requiring proper‑time evolution.

---

## Structural Role

Quanta provide the short‑lived, interaction‑mediating carriers of the
ontology. They:

- propagate only in modal time,
- enforce causal consistency between particle states,
- carry energy through waypoint density,
- and participate in at most two events, giving them finite causal chains.

They serve as the connectors between particle states in both possibility
and actuality.

---

## Relations

- Quanta are one of the two carrier classes, the other being particles.
- Quanta have ray bundles in possibility $P$ and minimal causal chains in actuality $A$.
- Modal time is their only progression parameter; they have no proper time.
- Actualization adds emission and absorption events to $\pi_q$.
- Pruning removes incompatible rays from their ray bundles based on particle interactions.
