# Realm

A **realm** is a mathematically defined domain with its own internal
structure, admissibility conditions, and ordering relations.

The universe contains two realms:

- the realm of actuality $A$, consisting of actualized events and their
  causal relations, and
- the realm of possibility $P$, consisting of admissible modal futures
  encoded as ray bundles and waypoints.

When no iteration index is written explicitly, $A$ and $P$ denote
generic instances of these two realms.

---

## Structural Role

Realms provide the top‑level ontological separation between:

- what **is** actualized ($A$), and  
- what **could be** in modal form ($P$).

This separation is essential for the dynamics of the P/A ontology:

- $A$ grows discretely through actualization.
- $P$ evolves continuously through modal progression.
- Actualization selects from $P$ and adds to $A$.
- Pruning removes incompatible futures from $P$ based on events in $A$.

Together, the two realms define the evolving universe
$$
U_n = (A_n, P_n).
$$

---

## Relations

- The **Universe** is the ordered pair $(A, P)$.
- **Actualization** acts across realms: it selects an admissible
  interaction from $P$ and produces an event in $A$.
- **Pruning** reshapes $P$ based on the newly actualized event in $A$.
- **Modal time** lives entirely in $P$.
- **Causal chains** live entirely in $A$.
- **Carriers** span both realms: they have ray bundles in $P$ and causal
  chains in $A$.
