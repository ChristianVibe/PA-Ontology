# Ray

A ray is a sequence of waypoints

$$
r = (w_0, w_1, w_2, \ldots),
$$

ordered by modal time. Each waypoint belongs to the carrier associated
with the ray, and the spacing between successive waypoints is determined
by that carrier’s intrinsic modal length.

---

## Structural Role

A ray represents one admissible modal trajectory of a carrier in the
realm of possibility. It provides:

- the ordered progression of waypoints in modal time,
- the geometric path along which compatibility is evaluated,
- and the structure through which modal futures become resolvable.

The intrinsic modal length of the carrier determines the waypoint
spacing, giving each ray a characteristic density and progression rate.

---

## Relations

- Each ray belongs to a ray bundle $R_c$ associated with a carrier $c$.
- Waypoints on a ray encode the modal state of the carrier at successive
  modal‑time steps.
- Actualization selects events when rays reach shared geometric
  intersections.
- Pruning removes rays that have no waypoint compatible with newly
  actualized events.
