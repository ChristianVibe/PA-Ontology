# Temporal Structure

Four distinct ordering structures appear in the framework:

- **modal time**, which orders waypoints along each ray in the realm of possibility;
- **proper time**, which applies to particles and arises from their spacetime embedding;
- **the iteration index $n$**, which orders global actualization steps;
- **spacetime time**, which is part of the coordinate structure of actualized events in $A$.

These ordering structures play fundamentally different roles. Modal time and iteration govern the dynamical evolution of the universe, while spacetime time is a relational parameter defined within the structure of actualized events. It does not determine the order of actualization, but encodes geometric relations among events once actualized.

Spacetime language is used for clarity, but spacetime structure is not taken as primitive. It is intended to be reconstructed from the causal structure of actualized events.

---

## Structural Role

The temporal structure ensures that the ontology does not rely on a single global time. Instead:

- **modal time** governs waypoint progression and compatibility in possibility,
- **proper time** governs particle dynamics in actuality,
- **iteration** governs the global ordering of actualized events,
- **spacetime time** provides geometric relations among events after actualization.

These distinct orderings allow possibility and actuality to evolve in coordinated but non‑synchronized ways.

---

## Relations

- Modal time lives entirely in the realm of possibility $P$.
- Proper time applies only to particles and orders their causal chains in $A$.
- The iteration index $n$ orders the evolution of the universe $(A_n, P_n)$.
- Spacetime time is assigned to events in $A$ after actualization.
- Carriers span multiple temporal structures: modal time for all, proper time for particles, and causal‑chain ordering for both particles and quanta.
