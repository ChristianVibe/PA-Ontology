# Actuality

Actuality is the set

$$
A = \{e_1, e_2, \ldots\}
$$

of actualized events. Each event is a pair

$$
e = (P_e, x_e),
$$

where:

- $P_e$ is the finite set of carriers participating in the event, and  
- $x_e$ is the spacetime point at which the event occurs.

No global time parameter is defined on $A$. Temporal structure in
actuality arises solely through the per‑carrier causal chains.

---

## Structural Role

Actuality represents what **is** realized in the universe. It contains:

- the discrete set of events,
- the carriers involved in each event,
- and the spacetime locations assigned to those events.

Actuality grows one event at a time through actualization. It provides
the causal backbone from which spacetime structure is reconstructed.

---

## Relations

- Actuality forms one half of the universe: $(A, P)$.
- Each event in $A$ extends the causal chain of every participating
  carrier.
- Actualization selects an admissible interaction from $P$ and adds a new
  event to $A$.
- Pruning reshapes $P$ based on the newly actualized event in $A$.
- Spacetime time is assigned to events in $A$ but does not determine the
  order of actualization.
