# Postulate 4 — Empirical Conservation

**ID:** post_empirical_conservation  
**Type:** Postulate  
**Category:** Empirical / Conservation

## Formal Statement

There exists a conserved quantity

$$
C : S \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^k
$$

such that for any transition $s \rightarrow s'$ produced by the transition rules in $L$, one has

$$
C(s) = C(s').
$$

This postulate expresses the empirical fact that physical processes respect conservation laws.  
It does not specify the structure of the transition rules; it only restricts which transitions are physically admissible.


---

## Interpretation

This postulate asserts that:

- Some quantity (or vector of quantities) C is conserved across all ontic
  transitions.
- Conservation is treated as an **empirical constraint** on allowed transitions,
  not as a fundamental dynamical mechanism.
- The ontology must forbid any transition that violates C(s) = C(s').
- The specific nature of C is left open: it may represent energy, momentum,
  charge, or other conserved quantities depending on the model.

In short:  
**Only transitions that preserve the conserved quantity C are physically allowed.**

---

## Ontological role

Empirical Conservation ensures:

- The ontology respects the empirical conservation laws observed in physics.
- Transition rules are constrained by physical admissibility.
- The global evolution Φ remains compatible with conserved quantities.
- Later postulates involving interference, nonlocality, and stable records
  operate within a conservation‑respecting dynamical framework.

This postulate provides a basic physical constraint on the allowed transitions
within the ontology.
