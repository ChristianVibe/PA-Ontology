# Postulate — Local Stable Records
**ID:** post_local_stable_records  
**Type:** Postulate  
**Category:** Empirical / Measurement  

---

## Formal statement

For every physical interaction that qualifies as a measurement in the
operational sense, there exists a site x in D and a discrete time step t such
that:

- **Existence:** Immediately after the interaction, a local record variable
  R(x, t) takes a definite value v ≠ ∅.

- **Stability:** There exist finite parameters τ > 0 and r ≥ 0 such that for all
  t' in the interval [t, t + τ] and all sites y with d(x, y) ≤ r, the local
  records R(y, t') remain operationally consistent with the value of R(x, t),
  except when altered by subsequent local interactions.

- **Operational accessibility:** The record value v is, in principle, accessible
  to other subsystems via interactions reachable from (x, t) under the
  finite‑speed dynamical constraints.

This postulate asserts only the empirical existence of stable, localized
records. It does not specify how or where ontic definiteness arises, nor does it
presuppose collapse, classicality, or global definiteness.

---

## Interpretation

This postulate asserts that:

- Measurements produce **localized, definite records** at specific sites and
  times.
- These records remain **stable** for a finite duration and within a finite
  spatial neighborhood.
- Other subsystems can **access** these records through ordinary interactions
  subject to finite‑speed constraints.
- The ontology does **not** assume any particular mechanism for record
  formation (collapse, branching, actualization, etc.).

In short:  
**Measurements leave stable, local, operationally accessible records.**

---

## Ontological role

Local Stable Records ensure:

- The ontology supports the empirical fact that measurement outcomes are
  definite, persistent, and locally accessible.
- Interference and which‑path phenomena can be described in terms of the
  presence or absence of such records.
- Later postulates involving interference, nonlocal correlations, and trigger
  conditions have a clear operational foundation.
- The ontology remains compatible with finite‑speed dynamics and local
  neighborhoods.

This postulate provides the empirical grounding for how measurement outcomes
appear within the ontology.
