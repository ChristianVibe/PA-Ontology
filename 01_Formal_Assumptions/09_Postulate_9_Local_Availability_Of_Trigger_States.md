# Postulate — Local Availability of Trigger States
**ID:** post_local_availability  
**Type:** Postulate  
**Category:** Structural / Interference  

---

## Formal statement

In any interference experiment, there exist physically admissible local states
of the experimental region in which the trigger condition for a distinguished
rule ℓ* (the interference‑relevant rule) is satisfied prior to detection. These
states are not exceptional: they form a subset of non‑zero relative cardinality
within the finite set of admissible local configurations of the experiment.

This postulate asserts only that trigger‑compatible states exist and are not
combinatorially negligible within the finite set of admissible local
configurations. It does not assert that the dynamics explores these states with
any particular frequency, nor that the local state space is sampled uniformly,
nor that the experiment is dynamically isolated from the rest of the universe.

It simply states that the trigger condition corresponds to a physically
admissible subset of local states, without making any claim about how often such
states are realized.

---

## Interpretation

This postulate asserts that:

- Interference‑relevant trigger states **exist** within the local state space.
- These states are **not rare** or measure‑zero; they occupy a non‑negligible
  portion of the admissible configurations.
- The ontology must allow the experimental region to enter such states.
- No assumptions are made about:
  - how frequently the dynamics visits these states,
  - whether the experiment is isolated,
  - or whether global correlations influence the local configuration.

In short:  
**Trigger‑compatible states must exist and must not be vanishingly rare.**

---

## Ontological role

Local Availability of Trigger States ensures:

- The ontology can support interference phenomena by allowing the relevant
  trigger condition to be satisfied.
- The interference‑relevant rule ℓ* is grounded in the structure of the local
  state space.
- Later assumptions about weak mixing and non‑fine‑tuned suppression have a
  clear structural foundation.
- The ontology avoids pathological models in which interference is impossible
  because the trigger condition is never locally realizable.

This postulate provides a structural requirement on the local state space in
interference experiments.
