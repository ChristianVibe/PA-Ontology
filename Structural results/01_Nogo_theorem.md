# Structural No‑Go Theorem for Undivided Actualization
**ID:** structural_nogo_undivided_actualization  
**Type:** Structural Result / No‑Go Theorem  
**Category:** Derived Constraint  

---
This document summarizes the main structural result of the P/A ontology: a statistical no‑go theorem showing that **locally triggered, unbounded global actualization rules are incompatible with the stable repeatability of interference**.

---

## Overview

The theorem applies to ontologies that adopt the structural axioms and postulates used throughout the P/A framework: stable local records, finite‑speed causal propagation, conservation constraints, and the empirical requirement of Tsirelson‑bounded correlations.

Within this structural space, the **Undivided Actualization Hypothesis (UAH)** asserts the existence of an actualization rule whose trigger is local but whose update domain is unbounded. Such a rule assigns definite values across arbitrarily large regions of the universe in a single step.

The no‑go theorem shows that **UAH‑type rules generically destabilize interference**, making its indefinite repeatability statistically impossible except on measure‑zero, finely tuned histories.

---

## The Undivided Actualization Hypothesis (UAH)

A rule satisfies UAH if:

1. **Local Trigger** — its firing depends only on the state of a finite region.  
2. **Unbounded Update Domain** — when it fires, it assigns definite values to degrees of freedom in arbitrarily distant regions.  
3. **Single‑Step Global Action** — the update is instantaneous at the structural level, not mediated by finite‑speed causal propagation.

UAH represents the structural analogue of collapse‑type rules with global reach.

---

## Core Mechanism

If a UAH‑type rule fires in the region where path coherence is established (e.g., near a slit barrier), its unbounded update domain assigns definite which‑path values across the entire interferometer. By the interference postulate, **any such assignment eliminates interference**.

Under the typicality postulates, trigger‑compatible configurations occur with nonzero probability in any physically relevant region. Over repeated runs, these occurrences cannot be systematically avoided.

Thus, interference is lost whenever the trigger fires in the coherence‑relevant region.

---

## Statistical Instability of Interference

Let $p_0 > 0$ be the probability that the trigger for the UAH rule occurs within the coherence region during a single run. Then the probability that interference survives across $N$ independent runs is at most:



$$(1 - p_0)^N$$

which decays exponentially as $N \to \infty$.

This shows that **UAH‑type rules are statistically incompatible with the observed stability of interference**.

---

## The No‑Go Theorem

**If an ontology satisfies the structural axioms and postulates and includes a UAH‑type actualization rule, then interference cannot remain stable across repeated experiments except on a set of measure‑zero, finely tuned histories.**

Equivalently:

> **Locally triggered, unbounded global actualization rules are structurally incompatible with the indefinite repeatability of interference.**

---

## Consequence for Ontology Design

Any physically admissible ontology within this structural class must therefore:

- **confine actualization to finite (or effectively bounded) domains**, or  
- **abandon local triggers**, or  
- **reject the operational assumptions of stable local records and finite causal speed**.

The P/A ontology resolves this tension by maintaining **bounded update domains** through event‑defined entanglement structure, avoiding UAH entirely.
