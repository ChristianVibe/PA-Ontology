# Wigner’s Friend — Conceptual Walkthrough (P/A Ontology)

In 1961, Eugene Wigner sharpened the measurement problem with a twist: imagine a Friend inside a sealed laboratory measuring a quantum system, while Wigner remains outside.  
For the Friend, the system has a definite outcome.  
For Wigner, the entire lab — Friend included — could still be described as a superposition until he opens the door.

The puzzle is whether “collapse” happens at the Friend’s measurement, at Wigner’s later observation, or not at all.

In the **Possibility/Actuality (P/A) ontology**, this apparent clash dissolves.  
There is no privileged observer, no dual realities, and no special collapse rule.  
Wigner’s thought experiment becomes a single, continuous chain of physical actualizations linking the quantum system, the Friend, and Wigner himself.

---

## Setup

Inside a sealed lab:

- The **Friend** measures a quantum system (e.g., photon polarization).  
- Outside, **Wigner** treats the entire lab (Friend + apparatus + photon) as a single physical system.

In standard QM:

- The Friend’s measurement “collapses” the photon’s state for them.  
- Wigner can still describe the lab as a superposition until he interacts with it.

---

## P/A Account

In P/A, there is no observer‑dependent collapse.  
There are only **actualization events** and **pruning of incompatible futures**.

The sequence is straightforward.

---

### Photon Enters the Lab

- The photon carries a ray bundle representing futures compatible with both “horizontal” and “vertical” outcomes.  
- At this stage, the photon’s polarization is the only degree of freedom whose ray bundle still contains multiple compatible futures.

---

### Friend’s Apparatus Interacts

- The polarizer and detector define a branching structure in **P**.  
- **Actualization selects one outcome**, fixing the photon’s polarization in **A** (Postulate 6).  
- By **Postulate 4**, all incompatible futures are pruned.  
- The detector’s state is now actualized accordingly.

---

## Von Neumann Chain Inside the Lab

- The detector’s actualized state interacts with the Friend’s sensory and neural systems.  
- These interactions produce further actualizations in the Friend’s physiology.  
- The Friend now has a definite record — not because “observation” caused collapse, but because **physical interaction committed the relevant degrees of freedom**.

---

## Wigner Opens the Lab

When Wigner interacts with the lab:

- He is **not collapsing a superposition**.  
- He is encountering a system whose relevant states were already actualized in a causal chain before he arrived.  
- His sensory systems undergo their own actualizations, correlating with the already‑committed states inside the lab.

---

## Why There Is No Paradox

### No dual realities
There is only one ontological history:  
the photon’s polarization was actualized at the Friend’s measurement.

### No privileged observer
Wigner’s later interaction does not change anything;  
it simply actualizes correlations in his own sensory systems.

### No superposed lab
The lab’s relevant degrees of freedom have been actualized since the Friend’s measurement.  
Any remaining openness concerns aspects unrelated to the photon’s polarization.

---

## Ontological Summary

Wigner’s Friend is not a conflict of perspectives but a **simple causal chain of actualizations**:

**Photon polarization → Detector → Friend’s physiology → Wigner’s sensory systems**

At each step:

- openness is pruned in the degrees of freedom that have interacted,  
- unrelated degrees of freedom continue to evolve,  
- no observer‑dependent reality emerges,  
- no special collapse rule is needed.

Just the same actualization dynamics that govern all physical interactions.
