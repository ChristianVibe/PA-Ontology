# Double Slit Experiment — Conceptual Walkthrough (P/A Ontology)

## Introduction — The Double Slit as an Ontological Testbed

Few experiments in physics carry as much conceptual weight as Young’s double‑slit experiment.  
In standard quantum mechanics, it illustrates wave–particle duality and wavefunction collapse.  
In the Possibility/Actuality (P/A) ontology, the same empirical phenomena appear, but the explanatory grammar is entirely different:

- no wavefunction  
- no duality  
- no observer‑dependent collapse  

Instead:

- A particle is always a particle.  
- What evolves is a **ray bundle** in **P** (structured possibility).  
- Interference arises from **local compatibility interactions** among rays.  
- Detection corresponds to **actualization** in **A**, which prunes incompatible possibilities and spawns new bundles.

The double slit is an ideal stage for seeing these primitives in action.

---

## Experimental Setup in P/A Terms

### 1. Emitter

**Standard role:** Emits one quantum entity at a time.  
**P/A role:**  
- Emission is an **actualization event** in A.  
- A **ray bundle** is spawned in P, representing the structured set of possible future trajectories.  
- The bundle propagates forward under local compatibility interactions.

---

### 2. Barrier with Two Slits

**Standard role:** Opaque screen with two apertures.  
**P/A role:**  
- The barrier imposes **geometric constraints** on ray propagation.  
- Rays intersecting opaque regions are **pruned** and recorded as **candidate absorption outcomes**.  
- Rays passing through the slits continue forward.  
- No actualization occurs at the barrier; it defines **possible outcomes**, not actual events.

---

### 3. Which‑Path Detector (optional)

**Standard role:** Determines which slit the particle passes through.  
**P/A role:**  
- A detector introduces a **candidate detection outcome** whenever a ray enters it.  
- If actualized, the event is written into A.  
- A new ray bundle is spawned at the detector location, carrying **definite path information**.  
- All rays incompatible with that event are **pruned**.  
- The detector does not collapse anything; it simply adds a new possible event that may be selected.

---

### 4. Receptor (Detection Screen)

**Standard role:** Records the arrival position of each particle.  
**P/A role:**  
- Rays reaching the receptor are pruned and added as **candidate detection outcomes**.  
- Local compatibility weighting determines their relative likelihood.  
- Actualization selects one ray, producing a single detection event in A.  
- Over many trials, the distribution reveals either an interference pattern or a single‑slit diffraction envelope.

---

## Why the Double Slit is Ideal for P/A

The experiment isolates the essential structural moves of the ontology:

- propagation of a ray bundle in P  
- geometric pruning at the barrier  
- introduction of candidate outcomes at detectors  
- respawning of ray bundles after actualization  
- local compatibility interactions producing interference  
- local geometric weighting producing Born‑like statistics  

Because the apparatus can be toggled between “with” and “without” which‑path detection, it cleanly contrasts:

- **modal openness preserved to the end**, versus  
- **modal openness pruned by an earlier actualization event**.

---

## Case 1: Without Which‑Path Detection

### Initial emission
- A particle is actualized at the emitter.  
- A ray bundle is spawned with compatibility for both slits.

### Interaction with the barrier
- Rays hitting opaque regions are pruned and recorded as candidate absorption outcomes.  
- Rays passing through either slit remain in the bundle.  
- No actualization occurs here.

### Post‑slit propagation
- The surviving rays form two overlapping lobes.  
- Local compatibility interactions steer rays, producing a scattering pattern analogous to interference.  
- No phase or wavefunction is used; the pattern emerges from local geometry.

### Actualization at the receptor
- Rays reaching the receptor are pruned and weighted by local compatibility density.  
- Actualization selects one ray.  
- Over many trials, the distribution matches the familiar interference pattern.

---

## Case 2: With a Which‑Path Detector at One Slit

### Initial emission
Same as before: a ray bundle compatible with both slits.

### Interaction with the barrier and detector
- Rays entering the monitored slit generate candidate detector outcomes.  
- If such an outcome is actualized:
  - The event is written into A.  
  - A new ray bundle is spawned at the detector.  
  - All incompatible rays are pruned.  
  - Only a single lobe continues.  
- If the detector outcome is *not* actualized:
  - Rays through the monitored slit are pruned.  
  - The bundle continues through the unmonitored slit as a single lobe.

### Post‑slit propagation
- In either case, only one lobe survives.  
- With no cross‑slit compatibility, no interference can form.

### Actualization at the receptor
- Rays reaching the receptor are pruned and weighted.  
- Actualization selects one ray.  
- Over many trials, the distribution matches the sum of two single‑slit diffraction envelopes — **no interference fringes**.

---

## Ontological Summary

- **Without which‑path detection:**  
  The ray bundle retains compatibility with both slits until the receptor.  
  Local compatibility interactions produce interference.  
  Actualization reveals the Born‑like distribution encoded in P.

- **With which‑path detection:**  
  The detector introduces a new candidate outcome.  
  If actualized, pruning removes all incompatible rays and respawns a definite‑path bundle.  
  Only a single lobe reaches the receptor, eliminating interference.
