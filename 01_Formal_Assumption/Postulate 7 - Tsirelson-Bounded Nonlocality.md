# Postulate — Tsirelson‑Bounded Nonlocality
**ID:** post_tsirelson_bounded_nonlocality  
**Type:** Postulate  
**Category:** Empirical / Correlational  

---

## Formal statement

There exist experimental configurations for which the observed CHSH correlation
can approach the Tsirelson bound 2√2 arbitrarily closely. Operationally, for
arbitrarily many trials, the empirical CHSH value S can approach 2√2 within any
tolerance δ > 0.

This postulate requires the ontology to support nonlocal correlations of
quantum strength, but not beyond. It also assumes that Bell‑type configurations
of the same operational structure can be instantiated with arbitrarily large
causal separation between the two measurement regions.

---

## Interpretation

This postulate asserts that:

- The ontology must reproduce **quantum‑strength nonlocal correlations**.
- The CHSH value S must be able to reach values arbitrarily close to **2√2**.
- Correlations stronger than quantum (super‑quantum or PR‑box‑like) are **not**
  permitted.
- Bell‑type experiments must remain possible even when the two measurement
  regions are separated by **large causal distances**.

In short:  
**The ontology must allow quantum nonlocality, but only up to the Tsirelson limit.**

---

## Ontological role

Tsirelson‑Bounded Nonlocality ensures:

- Compatibility with empirical violations of Bell inequalities.
- Support for nonlocal correlations arising from the ontic structure rather than
  signaling.
- That the ontology remains within the operational bounds of quantum theory.
- Coherence with other postulates involving locality, finite‑speed dynamics,
  and stable records.

This postulate provides one of the key empirical constraints shaping the
structure of the ontology.
