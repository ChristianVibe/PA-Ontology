# Postulate — Finite‑Speed Local Dynamics
**ID:** post_finite_speed  
**Type:** Postulate  
**Category:** Empirical / Locality  

---

## Formal statement

Local dynamical influences capable of transmitting controllable signals
propagate no faster than a finite constant v_max. This postulate constrains
signaling dynamics only; it does not forbid primitive ontic dependencies or
non‑signaling structural constraints that may be nonlocal in form.

---

## Interpretation

This postulate asserts that:

- Controllable signals have a **finite maximum propagation speed**.
- The ontology must respect a form of **operational locality**.
- Nonlocal ontic structure is **not excluded**, as long as it cannot be used
  for controllable signaling.
- The bound v_max applies to **dynamical influences**, not to correlations or
  structural constraints.

In short:  
**No controllable signal can propagate faster than v_max, but nonlocal structure may still exist.**

---

## Ontological role

Finite‑Speed Local Dynamics ensures:

- Compatibility with relativistic causal structure.
- A clear distinction between:
  - **signaling influences** (speed‑limited), and  
  - **non‑signaling correlations** (which may be nonlocal).
- A foundation for later postulates involving stable records, interference,
  and Tsirelson‑bounded nonlocality.
- That the ontology respects operational causality even if the underlying
  ontic state includes nonlocal dependencies.

This postulate provides the basic causal constraint on how controllable
influences propagate within the ontology.
