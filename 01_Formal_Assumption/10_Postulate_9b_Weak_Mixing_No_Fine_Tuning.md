# Subpostulate — Weak Mixing / No Fine‑Tuned Suppression
**ID:** subpost_weak_mixing  
**Type:** Subpostulate  
**Category:** Structural / Typicality  

---

## Formal statement

Across repeated runs of the same experiment—understood as distinct realizations
within the global history of the universe—the local state is not systematically
constrained to avoid the trigger‑compatible states. Even after many consecutive
runs in which the trigger has not fired, there remains a fixed, non‑zero lower
bound on the probability that the next run will enter a trigger‑compatible
state.

Histories in which the trigger is avoided indefinitely require fine‑tuned global
coordination and are therefore excluded as non‑typical.

This subpostulate does not deny the possibility of global correlations or
long‑range dependencies in the dynamics. The behavior of a local experiment may
depend on distant regions of the universe, including other laboratories
performing similar experiments. It rules out only the extreme case in which the
global state of the universe is coordinated so that trigger‑compatible local
states are never realized across arbitrarily many repetitions.

In other words, it excludes finely tuned superdeterministic histories in which a
physically admissible local configuration is suppressed across all repetitions.
It does not assume local autonomy; it merely rules out perfect, fine‑tuned
global avoidance.

---

## Interpretation

This subpostulate asserts that:

- Trigger‑compatible states must remain **accessible** across repeated runs.
- The ontology must not enforce **perfect, indefinite suppression** of such
  states.
- Histories that avoid trigger‑compatible states forever are considered
  **non‑typical** and require **fine‑tuned global coordination**.
- Global correlations and long‑range dependencies are **allowed**, as long as
  they do not produce perfect suppression.

In short:  
**Trigger‑compatible states cannot be avoided forever unless the universe is fine‑tuned to do so.**

---

## Ontological role

Weak Mixing / No Fine‑Tuned Suppression ensures:

- The ontology avoids pathological, superdeterministic histories that suppress
  interference‑relevant trigger states indefinitely.
- The Local Availability of Trigger States postulate has **operational teeth**:
  availability must translate into **non‑zero recurrence** across runs.
- The ontology supports typicality assumptions needed for the statistical
  no‑go theorem.
- Global sensitivity is allowed, but **perfect global coordination** is ruled
  out.

This subpostulate provides the typicality condition required to prevent
fine‑tuned avoidance of physically admissible states.
