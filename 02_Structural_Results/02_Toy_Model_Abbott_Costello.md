# Toy Model: Abbott and Costello’s Interferometers

This toy model provides an intuitive illustration of the structural mechanism behind the no‑go theorem for Undivided Actualization. It is not a physical claim about real laboratories, but a conceptual demonstration of how a locally triggered but globally acting actualization rule destabilizes interference in independent subsystems.

---

## Scenario

Two fictional experimentalists, **Abbott** and **Costello**, operate distant interferometers arranged so that:

- no controllable signal can propagate between them before detection,  
- their experiments share no entanglement, communication, or causal coupling,  
- the setups are structurally identical and run simultaneously.

This creates a clean setting for analyzing the consequences of a UAH‑type actualization rule.

---

## The UAH Rule

Assume the ontology includes an actualization rule $\ell_{\mathrm{UAH}}$ satisfying:

- **local trigger** — depends only on Abbott’s local apparatus state,  
- **unbounded update domain** — assigns definite values across arbitrarily distant regions, including Costello’s laboratory,  
- **single‑step global action** — updates all affected degrees of freedom at once.

By universality and observer‑independence, the rule’s trigger is local, but its update domain is global.

---

## Mechanism

Let $p_0 > 0$ be the probability that the trigger for $\ell_{\mathrm{UAH}}$ fires in Abbott’s apparatus *before* Costello’s detection.

Whenever this happens:

- the global update domain assigns definite which‑path values to Costello’s interferometer,  
- which‑path information eliminates interference by the interference postulate,  
- Costello’s experiment loses interference in that run.

Thus, the probability that Costello observes clean interference in a given run is at most:

$$(1 - p_0).$$

Over $N$ independent runs, the probability that *all* runs remain clean is:

$$(1 - p_0)^N,$$

which decays exponentially as $N \to \infty$.

---

## Interpretation

This toy model shows that:

- a locally triggered but globally acting actualization rule  
- generically spoils interference in independent experiments  
- unless the universe is fine‑tuned to avoid trigger‑compatible states.

The full no‑go theorem extends this mechanism from laboratory scale to cosmological scale using the structural and statistical assumptions of the P/A framework.

---

## Role in the Structural Results

This model is not part of the theorem’s proof.  
It is a **conceptual illustration** of the structural tension underlying the no‑go result.

It demonstrates, in a simple and memorable way, why **UAH‑type rules are incompatible with the stable repeatability of interference**.
