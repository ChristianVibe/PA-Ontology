# Axiom 2 — Universal Applicability
**ID:** ax_universal_applicability  
**Type:** Axiom  
**Category:** Structural / Dynamical  

---

## Formal statement

The transition rules in L are universal: each rule applies in the same way
wherever its applicability conditions are satisfied. The laws do not vary
across sites or subsystems.

---

## Interpretation

This axiom states that:

- The rules governing ontic evolution are **site‑independent**.
- A rule ℓ behaves the same way no matter **where** in the domain D it is applied.
- There are **no special regions**, privileged subsystems, or context‑dependent exceptions.
- The ontology’s dynamics are **uniform** across space and subsystems.

In short:  
**If a rule can fire somewhere, it fires in the same way everywhere.**

---

## Ontological role

Universal Applicability ensures:

- **Consistency** of the dynamical laws across the entire ontology.
- **Homogeneity** of the causal structure: no hidden “special zones.”
- **Compatibility** with locality and finite neighborhoods, since triggers depend only on local ontic variables.
- A foundation for later assumptions involving stable records, interference, and nonlocal correlations.

It is one of the core structural axioms that guarantees the ontology behaves
as a single coherent system rather than a patchwork of region‑specific rules.
