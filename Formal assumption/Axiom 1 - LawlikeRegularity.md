# Axiom 1 — Lawlike Regularity
**ID:** ax_lawlike_regularity  
**Type:** Axiom  
**Category:** Structural / Dynamical  

---

## Formal statement

There exists a finite set L of transition rules. Each rule ℓ in L acts on a
finite subset U ⊂ D (referred to as an ontic subsystem). The subsystem U may be:

- a bounded local neighborhood in the induced causal structure,
- a finite correlated subsystem,
- or, in special cases, the entire universe.

Each rule specifies (possibly stochastically) how the configuration on U changes
from one time step to the next, based only on the current configuration of U.
Repeated application of the rules in L generates the actual history.

Although no primitive global update rule is assumed, the composite effect of the
rules in L induces an effective global stochastic evolution

    Φ : S → Δ(S)

This ensures that models with undivided or globally coherent dynamics, including
those resembling unitary evolution, are included within the framework defined by
this axiom. In particular, the axiom does not exclude such models a priori;
rather, it permits them as special cases (including the case U = D) arising from
the composition of local rules.

---

## Interpretation

This axiom states that:

- The ontology is governed by a finite set of transition rules L.
- Each rule acts on a finite ontic subsystem U.
- Rules may be deterministic or stochastic.
- Global evolution Φ is emergent from the composition of local rules.
- Fully global, coherent dynamics (e.g. unitary-like evolution) are allowed as special cases.

---

## Ontological role

- Defines what counts as a “law” in the discrete causal formalism.
- Grounds the idea that local structure generates global behavior.
- Provides the dynamical backbone for other axioms and postulates (e.g. conservation, finite-speed dynamics, interference, nonlocal correlations).
