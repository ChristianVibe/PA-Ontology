# Postulate — Quantum Interference
**ID:** post_quantum_interference  
**Type:** Postulate  
**Category:** Empirical / Interference  

---

## Formal statement

There exist experimental configurations (such as double‑slit or interferometric
setups) such that:

- **Interference:** When no which‑path information is present in any subsystem,
  the detection statistics exhibit interference. These statistics cannot be
  represented as a convex mixture of mutually exclusive path alternatives.

- **Which‑path destruction:** When the setup is modified so that which‑path
  information is recorded in some subsystem, the detection statistics no longer
  exhibit interference and are well described as a convex mixture over definite
  path alternatives.

In this postulate, “which‑path information” means that some subsystem carries a
stable local record of the particle’s path, in the operational sense defined in
the Local Stable Records postulate. No assumption is made about how such a
record arises, and it need not be tied to any particular actualization
mechanism.

---

## Interpretation

This postulate asserts that:

- The ontology must reproduce **interference patterns** when no which‑path
  information exists.
- The ontology must reproduce **interference destruction** when which‑path
  information is present.
- The distinction between interference and non‑interference is grounded in the
  presence or absence of **stable local records**.
- The ontology does not assume any specific mechanism for record formation
  (collapse, branching, actualization, etc.).

In short:  
**Interference occurs when no subsystem records the path; interference disappears when a record exists.**

---

## Ontological role

Quantum Interference ensures:

- Compatibility with empirical interference phenomena.
- A clear operational link between record formation and the loss of
  interference.
- Coherence with the Local Stable Records postulate.
- A foundation for later structural assumptions involving trigger conditions and
  interference‑relevant rules.

This postulate provides one of the central empirical constraints shaping the
structure of the ontology.
